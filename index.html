Pre-seismic TEC Correlation

For my senior thesis at Eastern Nazarene College, I worked on earthquake forecasting research. It has been shown in previous research that several physical phenomena, which we call precursors, have anomalous behavior prior to large scale seismic activities. One such precursor is Total Electron Content. In this study, we examine TEC data from 2014 during certain days near and on the date of the 6.0 Mw Napa Valley earthquake in California, USA. We show by adaptively truncated Hotellingâ€™s T2 test that TEC in the Napa region for a known non-seismic baseline is statistically different than the TEC during several weeks surrounding the earthquake. This suggests a potential correlation between atmospheric TEC and major seismic activity. This work is currently submitted for publication.

Automated Software Performance Test Analysis

During the summer of 2018, I was hired as an intern at HCL Technologies in the software development division. The team that I was a part of developed tools to make software performance testing easier and faster. More specifically, as software development moves to a more left-shifted, continuous deployment model, there is a necessity for more testing. However, analysis of test data takes a significant amount of time and man-power, and with the increasing amount of testing, analysis is not always possible. During this summer, I developed a neural-network based algorithm to help automate and speed up analysis of software performance test results. The tool helps to highlight specific areas of performance test data which require attention and further analysis, thus reducing the volume of test data that engineers must parse. The algorithm that I developed during this summer is currently being patented and productionized by the company.

Data Aquisition Platorm

In the summer of 2017, I was a part of a research team working on developing a platform for earthquake forecasting. My portion of the project was to develop a central platform that would collect the data, and to create a server onto which this data could be transmitted for later analysis. The platform consisted of a raspberry pi with an adafruit ADC. Data was recorded, processed, and uploaded by python script that runs on the pi. Data was transmitted to an InfuxDB database running on a CentOS server hardened to CIS benchmarks. The platform will eventually be deployed in remote locations along fault lines to collect metrics related to earthquakes.

Simulation of Piezo-magnetic Potential

In the summer of 2016, I was a part of a research team working on developing a platform for earthquake forecasting. Part of my portion of the project was to research the theory behind piezo-magnetism and how it relates to pre-seismic magnetic anomalies. As part of this research, I developed a MATLAB simulation based on calculations of piezo-magnetism developed in Sasai (1980). The simulation allowed the input of various relevant fault parameters and outputed a map of the resulting piezo-magnetic field.
